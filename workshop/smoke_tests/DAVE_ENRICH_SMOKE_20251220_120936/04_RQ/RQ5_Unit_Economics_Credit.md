## Executive Summary

This report provides detailed analysis of Dave Inc.'s monetization mechanics, credit performance, and regulatory classification risk relative to industry peers. Dave operates with a gross monetization rate of 6.4% (Q3 2025) on ExtraCash advances, netting to 4.8% after 121-day credit losses. The company's effective pricing structure, when annualized, exceeds traditional credit card APRs but remains below payday lending rates. Regulatory reclassification of Earned Wage Access (EWA) as consumer credit under TILA represents a material risk to the current business model.

## 5. REGULATORY CLASSIFICATION RISK

### CFPB Proposed Rulemaking (July 2024)

**Key Provisions:**
- EWA products would be classified as consumer credit subject to TILA
- Tips and express fees would be considered "finance charges"
- APR disclosure required for all EWA products
- 14-day rescission right for consumers

**Impact on Dave:**
- Would require APR disclosure of 130-234% depending on advance terms
- Marketing claims ("not a loan") would be prohibited
- Potential reputational damage from high APR disclosure

### State-Level Regulatory Divergence

**States Classifying EWA as Credit:**
- California: Assembly Bill 1089 (2023) treats EWA as credit
- New York: DFS guidance considers tips/fees as interest
- Illinois: Pending legislation to regulate EWA

**States Exempting EWA:**
- Nevada: Explicit exemption from lending laws
- Missouri: EWA excluded from consumer credit definitions
- Florida: No specific guidance (generally permissive)

**States with Specific EWA Licensing:**
- As of Q4 2024, 12+ states require specialized EWA licensing
- Compliance costs: $2-3M annually per management estimates

### FTC/DOJ Lawsuit Implications

**Case 232-3014 Allegations (Filed November 2024):**
- Deceptive advertising of "tips" as voluntary when algorithms suggested amounts
- Failure to disclose true cost of borrowing
- Unfair practices related to automatic debit authorization

**Potential Outcomes:**
1. **Consent decree with behavioral remedies:** Most likely outcome based on precedent
2. **Monetary penalty:** $10-50M range based on MoneyLion ($1.75M) and Brigit ($18M) settlements
3. **Business practice changes:** May require elimination of express fees or other changes
4. **Industry-wide rulemaking:** Could accelerate CFPB classification
