### C. Channel Conflict Management

#### SaaS Partner Program Conflict Dynamics

**Channel Conflict Sources:**
- Lead/deal registration disputes (multiple partners submitting same prospect)
- Direct sales team vs. partner competition
- Territory/segment overlap
- Revenue attribution disputes
- Partner churn due to perceived unfairness

**Best Practices from HubSpot/Salesforce/Zendesk:**

**HubSpot:**
- NO native Partner Relationship Management (PRM) product (unlike Salesforce's expensive Experience Cloud)
- Channel conflict in deal registration: System must check via API if company exists, lifecycle stage, ownership before accepting partner submission
- If owned by internal rep or another partner, block submission with error message
- First-meeting-within-window rule for dual submissions, or split by segment/solution
- Tools like Introw codify rules so operations doesn't referee edge cases weekly
- Real-time visibility and automated attribution eliminate manual conflict resolution

**Salesforce:**
- 5,995 partners in ecosystem
- Experience Cloud for custom partner portals (expensive but robust)
- Automated deal registration prevents partner competition for same opportunities
- Fair reward distribution via accurate attribution

**Zendesk:**
- 1,251 partners
- Knowledge management and support tools for partner resources
- Community forums for partner-to-partner connection
- Focus on two-way communication and efficient support

Source: [Introw HubSpot Partner Management](https://www.introw.io/blog/the-3-ways-to-manage-your-b2b-partners-in-hubspot-and-attribute-revenue), [Journeybee HubSpot Partner Strategies](https://www.journeybee.io/resources/hubspot-partner-management), [ZINFI HubSpot PRM](https://www.zinfi.com/blog/partner-relationship-management-hubspot), [Softr Partner Portal Software](https://www.softr.io/blog/partner-portal-software)

**ZENVIA FRANCHISE CONFLICT RISKS:**
- NO PUBLIC DOCUMENTATION of conflict resolution policies
- Franchise model (vs. traditional SaaS partners) creates HIGHER conflict risk due to territorial exclusivity expectations
- 30 franchisees + direct sales = HIGH potential for overlap in Brazil market
- Lack of native PRM infrastructure (HubSpot doesn't have one; Zenvia unlikely to either)

**ASSESSMENT:** Channel conflict is UNRESOLVED RISK for Zenvia franchise scaling. Without published policies and automated systems, conflicts will require manual intervention, slowing growth and increasing partner churn risk.

## SUB-DRIVER 3: ENTERPRISE CUSTOMER ACQUISITION

### A. SMB-to-Enterprise Transition Case Studies

#### Zendesk (2012-2016)

**Transition Strategy:**
- Started with Atlassian-style inbound sales: "No sales people at all for first year" (Amanda Kleha, former SVP)
- No sales team until $10M run rate
- 2011: Launched first enterprise plan with "enterprise-grade features"
- "Land and expand" model: Sell to users first, expand to employing businesses
- 2017: SMBs = 70% of sales
- 2024: Enterprise = >50% of revenue (MAJOR SHIFT)
- 2024: 39% of revenue from $250K+ deals vs. 29% in 2020

**ACV Metrics:**
- Enterprise definition: ≥100 agents AND $50K ACV
- Early ACV: $300-400 (bi-modal with $10K tier)
- Moved beyond <$1K ACV customers while retaining SMB base
- "Volume is online but revenue is certainly more enterprise" (Kleha)
- Still thousands of SMB customers while scaling enterprise

**Sales Cycle and CAC:**
- Longer sales cycles and higher CAC when moving upmarket
- Offset by lower churn, higher upsell/cross-sell potential → best-in-class LTV/CAC ratio
- Longer buying cycles change marketing ROI, time to cover CAC
- Adapted pricing to offset cycle extension

**Revenue Milestone:**
- Achieved $1B annual revenue goal (set in 2016 by CEO)

### C. LATAM Enterprise Software Buying Behavior

#### Data Residency and Regulatory Requirements

**Brazil LGPD (General Data Protection Law, effective 2020):**
- NO strict data localization mandate
- Requires "adequate technical and administrative measures for data protection"
- Cross-border transfers allowed ONLY if receiving country ensures adequate protection
- Consent, contracts, or legal provisions must justify international transfers
- ANPD (national authority) can investigate and fine non-compliance
- Classified State information or Federal Public Administration data: May have residency/localization requirements

**Financial Sector Specifics:**
- CMN Resolution 4,893/2021: Financial data need NOT be stored in Brazil
- BUT financial institutions must ensure Brazilian Central Bank access to data
- Access supported by cooperation agreement between Brazilian Central Bank and foreign supervisory authority
- Banking information requires extra protections and specific consent mechanisms
- Many banks create dedicated Brazilian data environments to simplify compliance

Source: [InCountry LATAM Data Residency](https://incountry.com/blog/data-residency-requirements-in-latam-brazil-mexico-and-argentina/), [Baker McKenzie Brazil Data Localization](https://resourcehub.bakermckenzie.com/en/resources/global-data-and-cyber-handbook/latin-america/brazil/topics/data-localization-and-regulation-of-non-personal-data)
