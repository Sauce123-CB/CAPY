# RQ5: D-1 CPaaS Divestiture Valuation Multiple Analysis
## Zenvia Inc. (NASDAQ: ZENV)

**Research Date:** December 23, 2025
**Base Case Assumption:** 0.8× revenue (BRL 340M proceeds from BRL 837M CPaaS revenue)
**Uncertainty Range:** 0.5× - 1.0× revenue (BRL 210M - 420M)
**Variance Impact:** BRL 210M ($38M) = 57% of current market cap
**IVPS Sensitivity:** 15-20%

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This research investigates the highest-uncertainty driver in Zenvia's valuation: the CPaaS business divestiture valuation multiple. Analysis covers (A) CPaaS transaction comparables from 2020-2025, (B) distressed asset sale discounts, (C) Zenvia-specific asset quality factors, and (D) management track record. Key findings indicate CPaaS transaction multiples have compressed significantly from peak levels (15-20× revenue in 2020-2021 to 3-5× revenue in 2024-2025), with gross margin quality driving substantial valuation premiums. Zenvia faces multiple risk factors including liquidity pressure, customer concentration (37% from top 10 customers), and buyer scarcity in LATAM markets, all of which suggest downward pressure on achievable multiples.

### A.4 Gross Margin Premium Analysis

#### **Correlation Between Gross Margin and Valuation Multiple**

**Key Finding:** CPaaS providers with higher gross margins (70-80%+) command substantial valuation premiums over low-margin (10-15%) messaging/voice pass-through businesses.

**Evidence:**

1. **Sinch Acquisitions Comparison:**
   - **Pathwire:** 79% gross margin → 7.0× revenue multiple
   - **Inteliquent:** 48% gross margin → 2.1× revenue multiple
   - **Premium:** 233% higher multiple for 31 percentage point margin improvement

2. **SaaS/Software Sector Benchmarks (Q4 2024):**
   - **>80% Gross Margin:** 7.6× median EV/Revenue
   - **<80% Gross Margin:** 5.5× median EV/Revenue
   - **Premium:** 38% valuation premium for high-margin companies

3. **Public SaaS Companies (Q2 2025):**
   - **>80% Gross Margin:** 105% premium to SEG SaaS Index; 7.2× median EV/TTM revenue
   - **<60% Gross Margin:** 3.5× median EV/TTM revenue
   - **Premium:** 106% higher multiple

4. **CPaaS Industry Insight:**
   - "The most accurate methodology to value a CPaaS provider is to apply the right multiple on its **Gross Margin**, which reflects much better the value created by the business." Revenue multiples are "not very relevant" due to pass-through cost structure.
   - Public market CPaaS valuations ranged from 19.7× to 1.5× revenue and 73× to 13× EBITDA (2021 estimates)
   - **To enjoy high valuation multiples, CPaaS providers must demonstrate strong capabilities to address higher value enterprise use cases with an omnichannel offering**

5. **Case Study - Rubrik:**
   - Gross margin increased from 69% (3Q24) to 78% (3Q25) = +9 pp
   - EV/TTM Revenue multiple nearly doubled from 7.8× to 15.2×
   - **Demonstrates outsized valuation impact from advancing into higher gross margin cohorts**

**Zenvia-Specific Application:**
- **CPaaS Segment:** 64% of revenue but only 48% of gross profit (9M FY23) → **Low gross margin business**
- **SaaS Segment:** 36% of revenue but 52% of gross profit → Higher margin business
- **Implication:** Zenvia's CPaaS assets likely fall into lower-margin category, suggesting multiples toward bottom of range

**Sources:**

## WORKSTREAM B: DISTRESSED ASSET SALE DISCOUNTS

### B.1 Distressed Sale Discount Framework

#### **Typical Discount Ranges**

1. **Private vs. Public Targets:**
   - **Average Discount:** 15-30% for private/subsidiary targets vs. comparable public companies
   - **Source:** Academic M&A research (Officer 2007); unlisted targets experience "significantly discounted valuation multiples"

2. **Forced Sale Scenarios:**
   - **Example:** Homeowner in foreclosure selling at 70% of market value = **30% discount**
   - **Distressed Asset Losses (August 2023):** $419M loans liquidated with $218M losses = **52% loss rate**

3. **Urgency Premium:**
   - **Conventional M&A:** Several months to years
   - **Distressed M&A:** Couple of weeks to couple of months
   - **Impact:** "Need to raise capital quickly grants buyer more negotiating leverage—resulting in reduced valuation"

**Sources:**
- [A deep-dive into distressed sale discounts in restructuring plans](https://www.grantthornton.co.uk/insights/a-deep-dive-into-distressed-sale-discounts-in-restructuring-plans/)
- [Financing, fire sales, and stockholder wealth effects of asset divestiture](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0929119916301304)

---

#### **Fire Sale Conditions**

**When Fire Sale Discounts Apply:**
1. **Financially constrained firms** with limited debt capacity, forced to divest under creditor pressure
2. **Core asset sales** expected to suffer liquidity discounts
3. **Sales to non-industry acquirers** requiring bigger liquidity discounts
4. **Small firms** with limited external capital market access

**Offsetting Factors:**
- Financing benefits at firm level can partially offset fire sale discount
- Industry-wide distress (vs. firm-specific) exacerbates discounts

**Sources:**
- [Liquidation discount: Assessing Value Erosion in Forced Sales](https://fastercapital.com/content/Liquidation-discount--Assessing-Value-Erosion-in-Forced-Sales.html)

1. **Default Surge:**
   - **2023 Defaults:** 153 companies (vs. 85 in 2022) = **+80% increase**
   - Highest default rate outside of Covid spike (2020) in seven years

2. **Debt Maturity Wall:**
   - Large share of speculative-grade debt maturing 2025-2026
   - **"Corporate debt cliff"** concerns: debt initially financed at low rates coming due at 3-4% higher rates

3. **Tech Sector Distress:**
   - **Transition:** Growth-at-all-costs → cashflow-centric operating model
   - Companies in breach of debt covenants, unable to pay trade credit, lacking cash flow for debt service
   - **Opportunity for investors:** "Acquire promising tech companies at a substantial discount"

### B.3 Buyer Scarcity Impact on Valuation

#### **Scarcity Value Dynamics**

1. **Multiple Bidders = Premium:**
   - "Scarcity value driven by supply and demand—limited supply → bidding war → higher valuation"
   - "Understanding scarcity value crucial for sellers; highlighting unique assets/market position attracts more bidders and justifies premium"

2. **Limited Bidders = Discount:**
   - "Without multiple bidders, valuations are more reasonable; firms avoid paying above-market premium"
   - **Lower Middle Market:** "Buyer pool is smaller; due diligence drags out timeline"
   - **LMM Valuations:** 5-8× EBITDA (vs. larger deals); "more reasonable due to nature of LMM"

3. **Bid-Ask Spread (2022-Present):**
   - "Sellers holding out for higher value offers no longer on table"
   - "Valuations from bidders reduced more than seller expectations, creating meaningful 'bid-ask spread' since Q4 2022"

**Sources:**

#### **Niche Market Challenges**

**Deal Sourcing Issues:**
- "Traditional methods miss niche, under-the-radar targets"
- "Every PE firm combing same databases → seeing same deals"
- **Strategy:** "Active outreach to find multiple strategic buyers → strategic auction → negotiate for premium"

**Lower Middle Market (LMM) Disadvantages:**

### B.4 Earnout Structures in Distressed Sales

#### **Earnout Prevalence and Structure**

1. **Typical Earnout Percentage:**
   - **Common:** 10-25% of purchase price
   - **Distressed/High-Risk:** 20-30% earnout "quite common"
   - **Extreme Cases:** 90-100% earnout for "distressed company a buyer can turn around"

2. **Earnout Period:**
   - **Typical Duration:** 3-5 years post-closing
   - **Payment Structure:** 2-3 installments tied to performance milestones

3. **Performance Metrics:**
   - **Most Common:** Multiple of future EBITDA
   - **Alternative:** Revenue-based, gross margin-based, operational milestones

**Sources:**
- [Everything You Need to Know About Earnouts](https://blog.acquire.com/everything-you-need-to-know-about-earnouts-in-an-acquisition/)
- [Earnouts in M&A Transactions](https://www.jw.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/Earnouts-in-M_A-Transactions-5.22.2020.pdf)

---

#### **Distressed Seller Benefits**

**Why Earnouts Work for Distressed Companies:**
- "Earnout provides distressed seller with **immediate cash at closing** while preserving opportunity to recover potential upside via future payment"
- **Valuation Bridge:** "Overcome 'value bridge' between buyer and seller expectations"
- **Risk Sharing:** Reduces initial cash outlay for buyer; aligns seller incentives with post-acquisition performance

**Key Mechanisms:**
1. **Deferred Consideration:** Fixed payment timing (e.g., 12/24/36 months)
2. **Earnout:** Contingent payment based on performance metrics
3. **Hybrid:** Combination of deferred and performance-based payments

**Sources:**
- [Acquiring a Distressed Business in the United States](https://www.pillsburylaw.com/en/news-and-insights/distressed-business-us-buyers-m-and-a.html)

### C.4 Regulatory Transfer Risk

#### **Brazilian Telecom Regulations (ANATEL)**

**Foreign Ownership Requirements:**
1. **Corporate Structure:**
   - Telecom service providers **must be companies organized under Brazilian law**
   - **Headquarters and administration must be in Brazil**
   - Foreign capital permitted since Telebras privatization (1998)

2. **Voting Stock Requirements (Pre-2021):**
   - Majority voting stock must be held by:
     - Individuals resident in Brazil, OR
     - Companies incorporated under Brazilian law with headquarters in Brazil
   - **Post-2021 Changes:** Non-domiciled persons previously required local holding company; regulations may have been liberalized but specific current requirements not detailed in search results

3. **Authorization Requirements:**
   - "Practice of telecom activities requires prior authorization from ANATEL"
   - ANATEL responsible for regulation, inspection, granting authorizations
   - Administrative sanctions for violations

**Sources:**
- [In brief: telecoms regulation in Brazil](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=0744b250-f44e-44c0-842e-215a0f963ebb)

#### **Data Residency and Localization**

1. **General Rule:**
   - **No general data residency/localization obligations** for most commercial data

2. **Exceptions Requiring Local Storage:**
   - **Classified State information** (not limited to personal data)
   - **Federal Public Administration data**
   - **Financial data (CMN Resolution 4,893/2021):** Does NOT require Brazil storage, but Brazilian Central Bank must have access (may require cooperation agreement with foreign supervisor)

3. **ANATEL Security Requirements (Resolution 740/2020, amended 767/2024):**
   - Guidelines for security in telecom networks/services
   - Protection of critical infrastructures
   - **Data Retention:** Telecom companies must retain and share certain non-personal data (network operations, usage) for regulatory/security purposes
