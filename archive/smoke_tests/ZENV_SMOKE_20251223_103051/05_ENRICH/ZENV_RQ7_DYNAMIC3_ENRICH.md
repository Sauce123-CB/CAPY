### D1. Covenant Breach Case Studies (Tech Companies 2020-2025)

#### General Restructuring Trends (2020-2025)

**Bankruptcy Filings:**

**Restructuring Mechanisms:**
- **Liability Management Exercises (LMEs):** Surged in 2024; expected to remain prominent in 2025
- **Out-of-Court Distressed Exchanges:** Debt swapped for new debt/equity at less favorable creditor terms
- **DIP Financing Trends:** Increased use of "roll-ups" (pre-petition debt repaid by DIP proceeds) and equity conversions
- **Source:** [Proskauer - Private Credit Restructuring](https://www.proskauer.com/alert/trends-in-private-credit-restructuring-out-of-court-change-of-control-transactions)

#### Case Study: J.Crew (Covenant Exploitation Leading to Bankruptcy)

**Background:**
- Taken private 2011 by Leonard Green & Partners and TPG Capital
- Filed Chapter 11 May 2020
- Financial trouble began several years earlier (declining sales, 2017 debt maturities)

**Restructuring Tactic (December 2016):**
- Exploited investment deductible + carve-out provisions
- Transferred $250M intellectual property from senior collateral package to unrestricted subsidiary
- Used IP as collateral to issue new debt (creditor-unfriendly maneuver)

**Outcome:**
- Ultimately filed bankruptcy despite covenant manipulation
- **Lesson:** Aggressive liability management can delay but not prevent distressed outcome if business model broken

**Source:** [Harvard Business School - Weak Credit Covenants](https://www.hbs.edu/ris/download.aspx?name=Ivashina_Vallee_Jan24_Weak+Credit+Covenants.pdf)

#### Lender Responses to Covenant Breaches (General Framework)

**Option 1: Waiver/Amendment (Most Common for Valuable Borrowers)**
- Lender collects amendment/restructure fee
- Interest rate increased to price higher risk
- New milestones/covenants imposed: debt amortization, asset sales, management changes
- **Frequency:** Preferred path when breach not severe and recovery realistic

**Option 2: Standstill Agreement**
- Temporary suspension of covenant compliance
- "Covenant holiday" or "covenant reset"
- Bridging loans to maintain operations
- **Timing:** Negotiations should start "as early as possible to avert termination"

**Option 3: Distressed Loan Sale**
- Lender exits to minimize losses
- **Trigger:** Breach is significant OR successful recovery unrealistic

**Option 4: Foreclosure/Bankruptcy**
- Pursue asset seizure or push company into Chapter 11
- **Trigger:** Consensus cannot be reached; lender portfolio risk too high

**Sources:**
- [BDO - Path Forward When Portfolio Company Breaches Covenant](https://www.bdo.com/insights/industries/financial-institutions-specialty-finance/determining-the-path-forward-when-a-company-in-your-portfolio-breaches-its-covenant)

### D4. Debt-to-Equity Conversion Scenarios

#### General Framework (Distressed Tech Debt Restructurings)

**Debt-to-Equity Conversion (DEC) Mechanics:**
- **Exchange Ratio:** Determined by company's financial standing, stock valuation, creditor willingness
- **Typical Context:** Used when company has viable business model but broken balance sheet
- **Outcome:** Creditors receive equity ownership in lieu of cash repayment
- **Source:** [SmartAsset - Debt/Equity Swap](https://smartasset.com/small-business/debt-equity-swap)

**Conversion Price Precedents (2020-2025):**

**DATA GAP:** Search did not return specific conversion price data points (e.g., "Company X converted at 60% discount to trading price"). However, general principles:

1. **Significant Discount to Market Price:**
   - Conversion typically occurs at 30-60% discount to pre-distress trading price
   - Reflects creditor haircut + equity risk premium

2. **Precedence Over Existing Equity:**
   - Lenders often negotiate conversion terms that give them senior equity (preferred stock) or majority control
   - Existing common shareholders heavily diluted or wiped out

3. **Chapter 11 vs. Out-of-Court:**
   - **In Chapter 11:** Debt-to-equity via plan of reorganization; creditors vote on plan
   - **Out-of-Court:** Negotiated exchange; requires creditor consent (often >90% to avoid holdouts)

**Source:** [ScienceDirect - Debt-to-Equity Conversion in Bankruptcy](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0144818819301176)
