### B. Covenant Breach to Equity Dilution

#### Covenant Breach Dynamics

**Financial Covenant Types:**
- EBITDA-based leverage ratios
- Interest coverage ratios
- Debt-to-equity ratios
- Cash flow-based ratios

**Market Conditions (2020-2025):**
- Higher interest rates + tightening credit conditions + inflationary cost pressures + softening revenues
- "EBITDA compression in nearly all industries"
- Ultra-high valuations of acquisitions in past 3 years → significantly higher debt
- "Increased risk of breaching debt covenants for many PE firms"

Source: [Proactive Measures: PE CFOs Can Steer Clear of Debt Covenant Breaches](https://www.alvarezandmarsal.com/insights/proactive-measures-how-pe-cfos-can-steer-clear-debt-covenant-breaches-shifting-market)

#### Consequences of Covenant Breach

**Immediate Consequences:**
- Penalty or fee charged by creditor
- Increase in interest rate of bond or loan
- Conditional waiver may force refinancing at **higher interest rate**

**Recovery Strategies:**
- Equity financing from PE firms or traditional banks to remedy financial standing
- Amended credit agreements with new rates and/or covenants
- Waive penalties for past defaults in exchange for new payment schedule

Sources:
- [Covenant Breach - Definition, Example](https://www.fe.training/free-resources/project-finance/covenant-breach/)
- [A Lender's Guide to Breach of Covenant Solutions](https://www.bdo.com/insights/industries/financial-institutions-specialty-finance/a-lender-s-guide-to-breach-of-covenant-solutions)

#### Venture Debt Terms for Tech Startups

**Typical Terms:**
- Interest rates: **SOFR + 6-9%** (typical range **7-12%**)
- Maturities: **2-4 years**
- Warrant coverage: **0.5-1.5%**
- Covenants tied to liquidity or performance

**Market Shift Post-SVB Collapse (2023):**
- Non-bank funds stepped in after Silicon Valley Bank collapse
- Underwriting tightening
- Pricing moving upward

**Dilution via Equity Financing:**
- Equity financing brings capital without repayment but **dilutes ownership**
- Introduces external influence over company decisions

### C. Prepackaged Bankruptcy Outcomes

#### Common Shareholder Recovery in Bankruptcy

**Priority Structure:**
1. Secured creditors (banks)
2. Unsecured creditors (suppliers, bondholders)
3. Preferred stockholders
4. **Common stockholders (last)**

**Recovery Outcomes:**
- Common stock in bankrupt company is "**likely to be worthless**"
- Common shareholders get "**residual claim**" - only what's left after all prior obligations paid
- "Oftentimes, a company is unable to pay all prior obligations in full, which **leaves common shareholders without payout**"
- "Most companies that file for Chapter 11 **cancel all existing shares** of common stock even if the business returns to profitability"

Sources:
- [Investor Bulletin: Bankruptcy for a Public Company](https://www.sec.gov/reportspubs/investor-publications/investorpubsbankrupt)
- [Bankruptcy & Stock Market Investing](https://public.com/learn/bankruptcy-and-how-it-affects-the-stock-market)

#### Prepackaged Bankruptcy Trends

**Growth in Pre-Negotiated Bankruptcies:**
- Pre-packaged, pre-arranged, and pre-negotiated bankruptcies **increased markedly from 2019 to 2020**
- Trend "likely to continue gaining traction" in 2025
- Benefits: Streamline process, reduce time in court, minimize business disruption
- Challenges: Ensuring fairness to all creditors

**Typical Outcomes:**
- Stand-alone restructuring: Pre-bankruptcy equity interests **eliminated**, pre-bankruptcy debt **converted into equity**
- Alternative: Plan sponsor injects new capital in exchange for equity
- Reduced time and costs vs. traditional Chapter 11
